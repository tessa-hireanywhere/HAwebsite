The agency had been outsourcing post to three different freelancers — three invoices, three timelines, three Dropbox folders. They wanted one full-time editor, in-house in feel if not in geography. Here's what happened.

01 — Client

What does the client's business do?

A 14-person independent creative agency in Soho, founded in 2017. Their work sits where brand campaigns meet content production — they pitch the idea, then they shoot, edit and deliver it. Roughly two-thirds of the roster is UK consumer brands (food & beverage, retail, household names), with the remainder split between hospitality groups and one large Australian fintech they've held since 2021.

The agency runs lean by design. No holding company above them, no procurement chain to satisfy. The founder writes pitches at 9am and is in the edit suite by 4pm. Annual billings are in the £3–4m range — punching well above their headcount, but increasingly stretched by their own success.

By late 2025, post-production had become the bottleneck. Briefs that should have shipped in a fortnight were going out in five weeks. The work was still excellent; the throughput wasn't.

02 — Hire

Who was hired, and what's the role?

A Senior Video Editor, hired full-time and embedded into the agency's existing post team alongside the founder and one in-house designer. Officially she reports to the founder; in practice she runs the edit calendar herself and pulls in the designer when she needs motion or grading support.

Her remit is unusually broad for a senior editor — by design. The agency wanted someone who could own delivery end-to-end rather than just sit in the cut.

Within six weeks, the founder had stepped out of the edit suite entirely. The freelance roster was reduced from three to one — kept on retainer for overflow on shoot weeks.

03 — Package

What's the salary they pay?

£1,400 per month, gross. Paid via Hire Anywhere's payroll, with all Philippine statutory contributions (SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG) handled on the agency's behalf. No employer NIC, no UK pension auto-enrolment, no holiday accrual juggling — the agency sees one monthly invoice and one all-in figure.

For comparison, the same calibre of editor in central London — seven years' experience, agency-trained, comfortable in both Premiere and After Effects — sits at £55,000–£62,000 base on the open market. Add employer NIC, pension and a desk in W1 and the fully-loaded figure clears £70k/year.

The agency runs the placement at roughly 24% of UK fully-loaded cost. Annual saving against their best London offer: £40,800. They've routed most of it into a quarterly profit-share for the team and a new colour-grading panel.

"We didn't go overseas to cut corners. We went overseas because the maths of hiring a senior editor in London had stopped making sense for a 14-person agency. The work's the same. The invoice isn't."

— Founder / Creative Director
04 — Background

What was her background before this?

She came to the role with seven years inside agency post-production — split roughly evenly between Manila-based agencies serving Australian brand clients and remote contracts with two London-based independents. Her reel at point of hire included work for an FMCG brand the agency itself had pitched against the year before; we flagged this to the founder during shortlist review.

She holds a degree in Multimedia Arts from De La Salle–College of Saint Benilde (a feeder school for Manila's creative agency scene) and is fully proficient in Adobe Creative Suite plus DaVinci Resolve. Her English is native-fluent — she trained on Australian briefs from her second year and has presented cuts to UK clients on Zoom for the last three.
